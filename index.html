About Me

I'm a recently earned a M.S. in Data Science at Boston University with a passion for using machine learning, natural language processing, and time series modeling to uncover insights and solve real-world problems. I earned my B.S. in Data Science from the University of Michigan, where I also pursued a minor in Asian Languages and Cultures. Throughout my academic and professional experiences, led projects ranging from consumer sentiment forecasting to building poker bots with deep reinforcement learning. I've also worked for NPR. I enjoy working across the full data pipeline—from cleaning and exploring messy datasets to building and evaluating predictive models.
